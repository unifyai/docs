Models#

One of the key concepts within the Hub is the model. In this section, we’ll explain what we mean by models and how they fit into the Hub ecosystem.

What is a Model?#

A model in the hub represents a specific machine learning system that has been integrated and benchmarked for informed and easy access and deployment.

Models are categorized based on their characteristics, modalities, and tasks. You can filter models according to your specific requirements, whether it’s latency, throughput, or cost. You can learn more about this in the benchmarks page.

Types of Models#

There are two types of models:

Models uploaded by the community#

When a user (which could be you!) uploads a model, we automatically create and benchmark a set of endpoints by deploying the model accross various providers.

The model-id of these models typically follows the format: <username>/llama-2-70b-chat.

Note

An interface to upload your own models will be available very soon, but currently, we are still testing this feature. If you want to publish your model right away, please send us a mail to hub@unify.ai!

Models uploaded by us#

In this case, we manage the model, serving as a reference point for particularly relevant models. In this type of models you will find endpoints and benchmarks from the same providers as in user-uploaded models. However, here you will also find public endpoints hosting the model.

In these cases, the model-id won’t have a username and will be simply formatted as llama-2-70b-chat.

Available Models#

The easiest way to explore the list of models in the hub is through the web interface. Here, you can simply search for the model you are interested in and click on it to visualise the benchmarks and all the relevant information about the available endpoints.

Round Up#

You are now familiar with the different types of models that are available in the Hub. In the next section, we’ll dive into the Providers and its role within the Hub!
